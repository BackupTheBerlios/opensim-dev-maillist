Folks, you may be missing the point... LL made the viewer source 
LGPL, changing the previous restrictive GPL.  That means that 
previous legal advice and inputs are not relevant any longer. LGPL is 
a good thing for us.  I tried to persuade the Project Wonderland 
folks they should go for LGPL for educators, but I don't think they 
really understood the deterrent of custom licences and the positive 
value of LGPL.

Note though that a lot of addons and modules needed to build the 
viewer still have their own licence term, and may not be as flexible 
as LGPL, some like Vivox are of course not redistributable at all.

http://hg.secondlife.com/viewer-development/src/00a831292231/doc/LICENSE-source.txt

I note Aurora already changed its name to Kokua...
http://imprudenceviewer.org/2010/11/01/announcing-the-kokua-project/
