Also the permissions manager is (or should be) designed so that other permissions managers can be created/used. So if someone has a requirement for a different behaviour on their region then they can use/implement another one. 

OpenSim isn't and can't be about implementing the permission managers (or any other part) for every use case. It is just a platform and a reference implementation.
 
 One problem is that anyone running a region could easily disable the whole permissions manager, if they wished. The only way around this, is really for the Grids/region owners to agree to some TOS/Contract. 
 
 That is what LL / SL does, there is nothing technical stopping them from disabling the permissions systems on whatever regions they want (or all of them). But they have agreed to honour a set of permissions. So their system is just based on Trust. The same system can be implemented in OpenSim, the grid/region operator just has to show that they can be trusted. 
 
 Like, if you were on a open grid that anyone could connect a region to. Then no matter what permissions manager was implemented, it wouldn't stop the region owner from doing whatever they want, to objects placed in their region. As they could just disable the permissions manager. 
 
OpenSim is not about creating any one grid/application. Its about creating a base platform that other people can take and adapt to whatever they want. So someone who wants a different permissions system on their grid, could implement one, then do various things to make sure all regions on that grid have it enabled. And show the users that it can be trusted.

"Frisby, Adam" <adam at deepthink.com.au> wrote: Parcel owners is a tenuous one, and I'd agree that permissions should be enforced there, although I can understand the logic behind "If it's on your parcel, you should be able to do what you want with it.".

However region operators I do believe should have access to anything running on their regions. The reason for this being simply that if the code is operating on their servers, they deserve the ability to observe what is going on, if you don't want it looked at - sign a contract with your operator enforcing that, or host it yourself. (This is nothing different to how ISPs and Webhosts have the ability to look at scripts running on your website account)
