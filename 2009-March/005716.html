+1 Melanie. Why are we trying to reinvent the wheel?

Since OpenSim, (also called the 3d web), is a server/service, similar to 
Apache or IIS, (the 2d web), can we just follow their example for 
configuration files? IIS has a web/gui based setup, which I assume is 
more than we are ready to do with Opensim at this stage. Apache has one 
config file, with embedded links to other config files as needed or 
wanted by the administrator. So, for those who want, one big config, for 
those who prefer multiple config files/folders, you can have it your way.

BTW, I have no Linux/MS bias here. I've been SysAdmin for both systems 
for a long time.

Peace,
Sean
