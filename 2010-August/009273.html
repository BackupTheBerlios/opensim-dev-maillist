Karen Palen wrote:
> As a suggestion,  the Image:PoweredByOpenSimulator.png 
> <http://opensimulator.org/wiki/Image:PoweredByOpenSimulator.png> image 
> might be added to the wifi welcome and splash screens in a way that 
> shows an example of the intended and correct use. This would not only 
> help educate users, but doing so establishes an "informal trademark".
> 
> Another thought is the possibility of making one or more of these images 
> an official registered trademark of the project.
> 
> Registering a trademark is not hard to do, but registration would 
> eliminate some potential abuses as "cybersqautting". For example this 
> would be where someone decides to use a close variant of the name/logo 
> in a malicious or confusing way such as registering the domain name 
> "OpenSimulator.com" (rather than .org) or even "ImprovedOpenSimulator.com".
> 
> The standard for a trademark is "possibility of confusion", the standard 
> for copyright (including BSD) is a "literal copy" or "derived work" 
> which is much narrower.
> 
> Karen

>                     -1 on this.
>                     I'm generally reluctant in using the term "OpenSim"
>                     for anything other
>                     than the name of this project. It's bad enough that
>                     bloggers and the
>                     public keep confusing the project with its uses,
>                     let's not give any
>                     more suggestions in that direction. OpenSim is the
>                     infrastructure
>                     project, not its uses. The users of OpenSim are the
>                     world operators,
>                     not the people who login to these worlds.
> 
>                     In other words, when people login to an
>                     OpenSim-based world they are not
>                     "logging in to OpenSim"; they are logging in to a
>                     world that someone
>                     runs based on OpenSim. This may seem like an
>                     insignificant distinction,
>                     but it's not, it's huge.
> 
>                     So let's leave it with the generic message that is
>                     has now. The config
>                     var is there for people to customize it.
