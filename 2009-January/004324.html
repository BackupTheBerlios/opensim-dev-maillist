Looking at the recent addition of hypergrid to OpenSim; great stuff,  
but...

I'd like to have external users only be able to access some of my  
grid, so there are private "local user only" parts, and a shared space  
for local and remote users. For example, I'm looking at using OpenSim  
in education, a major advantage of which is no age restrictions, but  
it does mean we want to be able to carefully control parts of the grid  
that students can wander unchecked.

Having been thinking about this, to me it would make most sense if we  
ran two grids. An external grid that runs in a standard hypergrid  
configuration, and an internal grid that refuses to accept avatars  
coming over the hypergrid connection, but can send them out. Does that  
sound sensible and feasible?
