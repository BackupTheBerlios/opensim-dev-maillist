Sean Dague wrote:
> Tonight I pulled together linkage between svn and mantis upon a request
> this afternoon.
> 
> For committers, it's pretty simple.  If you include the string "mantis
> #1234" somewhere in a commit, it will append the text of that commit
> into that mantis item as an update.  If you include the string "fixes
> mantis #1234" it will add that to the mantis, and mark it as RESOLVED /
> FIXED.
> 
> This should make life a little simpler in closing mantis items.
> 
> 	-Sean

Okay, I made the mistake of not reading sdague's e-mail carefully enough and just putting "fixes mantis #1234" in the 
commit log entry.  Of course, this is cool in updating the mantis but it would also be very good to continue separately 
crediting the contributor in the svn log since this is the only practical way to see who is contributing patches.

Of course, probably everybody else is aware of this and it's just my dumb self who only put the string "fixes mantis 
#1234 without any accompanying text).
