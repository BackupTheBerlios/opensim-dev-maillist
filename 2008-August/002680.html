You  bring up an interesting concern of mine, SDague, and that is daylight savings time.

Currently our "Office Hour" and "Test Hour" are at 1900UTC which is Noon PDT and 3PM EDT.

However, when daylight savings time ends in most of the US in another month or so, things will change and we have two choices.

1. Keep "Office Hour" and "Test Hour" at 1900UTC and let those on daylight savings time adjust to 1100PST and 3PM EST.

2. Change the time from 1900UTC to 2000UTC and follow daylight savings time twice a year.

My vote is to keep our "Office Hour" and "Test Hour" at 1900UTC as we are a planetary project and this seems more reasonable for the folks that dont use daylight savings time.

But, what to others think?

Charles

As we are always playing the "when does the meeting actually start?"
game, and the "oh right, that's now?" game, I thought it might be useful
to create a public google calendar with the meetings on it.  It is
available as:

HTML:
http://www.google.com/calendar/embed?src=v6e2o6vtc3mt9lv9ladn420q7k%40group.calendar.google.com&ctz=America/New_York
(that will give you my TZ, you can adjust yours appropriately, the
callendar is in GMT).

iCAL:
http://www.google.com/calendar/ical/v6e2o6vtc3mt9lv9ladn420q7k%40group.calendar.google.com/public/basic.ics

Thunderbird + Lightning + Google Calender connector makes this rather
seamlessly work on any platform, though iCAL is pretty standard, and
works on a lot of different mail clients as well.
